Software Craftsman


What type of developers are you looking for?

We are looking for developers with the following mindset:

You consider yourself a software crafter. You care about and are proud of the code you write. You care about doing meaningful work and providing value to clients via well-crafted code. For you, writing code is not just a job but also a passion.

Test-Driven Development is second nature to you and that's how you write code. You'd don't use "lack of time" as an excuse to do a bad job. You love pair programming and that is your preferred way to write code.

As a passionate developer, you spend a considerable amount of time outside working hours investing in your career, learning new technologies, practicing different techniques and approaches, and above all, sharing what you know with others. You don't do it because a client or employer asks you to; you do it because you want to.

You probably have a blog, some code on GitHub, and frequently attend technical community events. You also probably have a pet project or contribute to open-source projects.

You understand that languages and frameworks are just tools. You choose the best tools for the job without being religious about it. The majority of books and articles you read are the ones that makes you a better developer and not a specialist in a specific framework. At the same time, you are extremely proficient with at least one or two programming languages and testing frameworks.

You understand and embrace Agile processes, and are able to help other people to understand and adopt them. You are able to work close to clients and Product Owners and help them to refine requirements and plan the work to be done.

Your are proud to be a developer and want to remain a developer. You believe in emergent and collaborative architecture and design; you use your knowledge of SOLID principles, the Four Elements of Simple Design, and Domain-Driven Design to design applications that reflect the business domain.

If you reached this point and believe that you satisfy all the criteria above, we are definitely interested to hear from you.


What is expected from a craftsman at Codurance?

As a craftsman at Codurance, you are expected to do many of the following activities:

  • Build well-crafted bespoke applications to clients.
  • Lead by example: You strive to work at your best, you don't cut corners, and you always behave with professionalism, regardless of which environment you are in.
  • Work with our clients, embedded in their teams, and help them to get better at delivering quality software.
  • Travel outside London, although we will always try to keep that to a minimum. The vast majority of our clients are based in London, but as a consultancy company, we may have clients anywhere in the UK or abroad.
  • Mentor apprentices: You will be responsible for helping them to become craftsmen. Although the biggest part of this mentorship will happen during working hours, be prepared to spend extra hours on this.
  • Become proficient in multiple languages and technologies.
  • Give something back to the community, writing blogs, sharing code, giving talks, running workshops, and participating in external technical communities, specially the London Software Craftsmanship Community.
  • Be humble, always respecting and helping developers regardless of their skill and experience, whether they are part of Codurance or not.

Before applying, make sure the requirement to travel and to work with diverse set of clients suits your preferred lifestyle.


What are the advantages of being a craftsman at Codurance?

  • Exposure to many different projects, clients, industries, and technologies.
  • Be part of an amazing team of craftsmen, which guarantees that you will always be learning.
  • Work for a company with a flat structure, with no managers, no architects, no team leaders, just awesome developers.
  • You are recognised and respected for being a great developer.
  • Flexible working hours. As long as there is no disruption to the projects and the team agrees, you can work from wherever you want.

What technologies do you use?

We use whatever is fit for purpose. We love trying new things and experimenting. The majority of our clients currently use Java and/or C#. Strong knowledge in at least one of the these is always an advantage.


I want to apply. What do I need to do?

First phase

We don't read boring CVs. If you think Codurance is for you, drop us a line at hello@codurance.com with the following information:

  • A brief description of who you are. Things we are interested:
    • How passionate you are about software
    • What software craftsmanship means to you
    • When you started coding
    • Technologies you are very comfortable with
    • Technologies you are currently learning
    • Technologies you would like to learn
    • Favourite technical books / bloggers
    • Technical communities you are an active member of
    • Conferences you attended or spoke at
    • Books or articles you wrote
    • Any pet projects you are working on (overall idea, technologies used, etc — relax, we won't steal your idea and won't tell anyone)
  • Link to your GitHub account (or BitBucket, Google Code, etc)
  • Link to your blog
  • Link to your Twitter account
  • Link to your LinkedIn profile
  • Links to any open source project you contributed
  • Links to any applications you built that are publicly available
  • Anything available online that can help us know more about you.

Second Phase

We are going to send you some requirements and you will need to implement a small application—slightly bigger than a kata. It can be done in a few hours, maybe a day. You choose which language you want to use.

Third Phase

If we liked your code, we will be inviting you in to meet us and to pair with us for a few hours. You will need to bring your own laptop with your favourite language and tools installed.
